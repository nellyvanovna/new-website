One hundred years of solitude

This is a free transposition of the magical realist masterpiece One hundred years of solitude written by Gabriel García Márquez. It talks about the multi-generational story of the Buendía family, and the conflicts that take place in Colombia.

Some unearthly, mysterious, or magical text extracts have been selected and gathered with real photographs of conflicts, that took place one hundred years ago all over the world, compared to nowadays. This work shows the universality of conflict, and denounces its absurdity.

Tino Seghal’s book

This book shows the working process of the contemporary artist Tino Seghal, who creates artistic performances which involves amateurs. He doesn’t allow visual reproduction of his work. This book is focused on his exhibition at the Guggenheim Museum in New York in 2010, where he presents two of his performances: This Kiss and This Progress.

The book is divided in two parts, one for each performance. A possible content of his personnal book is imagined by analysing his work, like photos of the Guggenheim Museum, choregraphic sketches, questionings, post-its with appointements, etc.

  • 58 pages
  • Soft cover, perfect bound

Morgane Fourey and materiality

It is an editorial and font project for the french artist Morgane Fourey. She designs projects with optical illusions on volumes, by making a parallel between what the spectator sees or never sees, like the work of an artisan or of an artist in workplace.

She plays with the tenuousness of materials by including them in her work thanks to her craftsmanship. This book presents her work by taking as main axis the materials she uses.

Encyclopedia of fortuity

The encyclopedia is one of the main masterpieces of the Enlightenment, which is supposed to be ordered and hierarchized. This work is taking the codes of a classical encyclopedia, and list all irrationally made discoveries: by accident, by a combination of circumstances, by mistake, by pure chance, inadvertently, by clumsiness, or by professional negligence.

The inner pages are interchanged according to a sequence of numbers generated randomly during printing, in order to create an intuitive and detached reading experience, like the fortuity we can’t control. Each book is printed with a different pagination, which makes it unique.

  • 204 pages
  • Hardcover, edition bound

Mitsuo Katsui stamps

The stamps and the booklet are realised as an effigy of the japanese colorist designer Mitsuo Katsui. He is mostly known for his very colored posters and for being in advance in his time.

He observes his environment, and take pictures of landscapes for colour dots, like a pointillist. Theses stamps resume his creative path while referring to his work as a graphic designer.

  • 22 pages

Discussion — History

This is an editorial project that speaks about USSR and Russia. This book contains two books, one of them is composed of a sensitive and personnal content, the other one is more analytical. They are connected. There is a dialogue with my mother who tells her experiences in USSR when she lived there and trimmings of my childhood photographs in Russia, like flashes of memory.

This trivial stories presents us and customs, social norms, fashion, decoration and life of a past world. The dialogue is in russian, and translated in french. The analytical part of the book helps with understanding of russian terms and gives a historical content. The cover is taking codes of a no- vel book, and the fusion of two books represents the do it yourself, very representative of the soviet era.
